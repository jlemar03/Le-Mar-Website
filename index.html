I have read many books in my career as a self proclaimed high-tier reader, and have used my venerable knowledge to create a list of absolute peak fiction. These books spread across many genres and formats, from niche chinese webnovels to renowned American classics. This list is meant to put you on books that, although might require more than a 1st grader's reading comprehension, are well written and have satisfying endings.

Top 5

  1. The Perfect Run

    Description:

    Ever wonder what it would be like to have the ability to save a moment in time? Now imagine that in a post-apocalyptic world with out of control gangs, superpowers, and corporations with more power than the government.

    Opinion:

    Honestly one of the best time travel examples I have seen, is easy to read and isn't too long.

    Rating:

    4.2 of 5 Stars

  2. Eragon

    Description:

    A young farmhand finds himself caught in the middle of a century old war after a routine trip into the forest near his village. With his home destroyed and now being hunted by the tyrannical king, he leaves his adopted family behind to travel lands unknown to him, trying his best to learn about the predicament he is now in.

    Opinion:

    May be a bit lengthy for new readers, with books around 600-800 pages. A great book for fantasy worlds with a rich history.

    Rating:

    3.9 of 5 Stars

  3. The Great Gatsby

    Description:

    The Roaring 20s' were one of the most exciting times in American history. America had just experienced the largest war ever, electricity is largely publicly used, and the economy is booming(for the upper class). As Nick steps into his new home, he learns how the 1% and those with old money live, and how unequivocally different their lives are to everyone else.

    Opinion:

    I liked how the class disparity was shown and all of the symbolism and imagery used. Provides an insight to what life was like during the 1920's.

    Rating:

    4 of 5 Stars

  4. The Alchemist

    Description:

    The Alchemist is a novel that tells the story of Santiago's quest for fortune, his Personal Legend, and the decoding of the Language of the World. The young man must endure many obstacles. First, he must overcome those who have told him since childhood that his dreams can never be realized. Santiago has to understand that those who truly love him will not begrudge him the pursuit of his dreams. Then, he must hold back fear of failure and defeat to continue his quest. Last, he has to understand that the worst thing in life is to deny one's dreams.

    Opinion:

    This is definitely a religious novel, but that makes impactful claims about the human condition and what it means to struggle. This isn't as action packed as some of the other books on the list, but definitely has tension and will make you feel something within your soul.

    Rating:

    4.4 of 5 Stars

  5. Shadow Slave

    Description:

    A young man in the Outskirts of society shows symptoms of the Spell, a mysterious sickness that grants strange and inhuman powers to those who can pass its cruel and vicious trials. Those that are well-off have the best training and equipment have the best chances to survive, those in the middle class have a 1 in 4 chance if they can receive some in school training, while for those on the Outskirts, it is practically a death sentence.

    Opinion:

    Great world building, fleshed-out and humorous characters, great character development, very intense moments and bone chilling moments throughout the entire novel. Checkov's gun.

    Rating:

    4.8 of 5 Stars

Honorable Mention

$100m Offers

Description:

Provides strategies for creating high-converting offers in digital marketing. It emphasizes the importance of targeting niche markets, increasing perceived value, and incorporating elements like guarantees and urgency. The goal is to design "Grand Slam Offers" that deliver maximum value at higher prices.

Opinion:

Although I myself have not been able to implement these tactics (no business), the lessons taught are timeless and, after you understand what they mean, seem like common sense.

Rating:

4.5 of 5 Stars
